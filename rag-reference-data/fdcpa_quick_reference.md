# FDCPA Quick Reference — Debt Collection Agents

## What is the FDCPA?

The Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (15 U.S.C. §§ 1692–1692p) is the primary federal law governing third-party debt collection in the United States. It applies to personal, family, and household debts including credit cards, medical bills, auto loans, and private student loans. It does NOT cover business debts.

## Mini-Miranda Disclosure

Every call must begin with: "This is an attempt to collect a debt. Any information obtained will be used for that purpose." Failure to deliver this disclosure is a federal violation.

## Communication Rules

- Permitted hours: 8:00 AM – 9:00 PM in the consumer's local time zone.
- Call limit: No more than 7 calls within 7 consecutive days per account.
- After a live conversation, wait 3 days before calling again on the same account.
- Do NOT contact a consumer who is represented by an attorney.
- Do NOT disclose the debt to third parties.

## Consumer Rights

- Consumers may dispute the debt in writing within 30 days of the validation notice.
- Upon written dispute, all collection must cease until verification is provided.
- Consumers may request the collector stop all communication in writing.

## Prohibited Conduct

- Threats of violence or criminal action.
- False representations about the debt amount or legal status.
- Calling outside permitted hours.
- Contacting consumers at work if the employer prohibits it.
- Using obscene language or harassment.

## Key Penalties

- Individual lawsuits: Up to $1,000 per violation plus actual damages.
- Class actions: Up to $500,000 or 1% of collector's net worth.
- CFPB civil penalties: Up to $50,000/day for knowing violations.
